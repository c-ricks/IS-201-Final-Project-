Welcome to the Marine Layer

Hello! If you found this page you are probably a huge Seattle Mariners fan just like me. You've also probably been asking yourself for years, how is the Mariners offense so much better on the road compared to at home and is T-Mobile Park really cursed?

History of the Marine Layer

The marine layer at the Marinersâ€™ ballpark (T-Mobile Park) plays a meaningful role in how the game is played, especially for hitters. Because the stadium is close to Puget Sound, cool and moist air often settles over the field, particularly during night games. This dense air increases drag on the baseball, which reduces how far balls travel when they are hit. As a result, well-hit fly balls that might be home runs in other parks often die out on the warning track in Seattle. This helps explain why T-Mobile Park has historically favored pitchers and why offensive numbers, especially power statistics, tend to be lower compared to warmer, drier ballparks.
